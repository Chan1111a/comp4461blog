Social Extended Reality (XR) for Local Community: Second Hand Furniture Platform

Description of the Project and My Goal

Our group project focuses on enhancing the experience of trading second-hand furniture through the integration of Extended Reality (XR) technologies, specifically Augmented Reality (AR) and Virtual Reality (VR). The platform we are developing allows buyers to preview furniture in an immersive and interactive way using AR, enabling them to visualize how items would look and fit within their own living spaces. This helps address common concerns such as size, style compatibility, and condition of the furniture. On the seller's side, the platform provides tools to scan and upload 3D models of their furniture into a virtual marketplace built with Unity. This virtual environment serves as a dynamic and engaging medium for showcasing items and facilitating transactions. My personal goal in this project is to explore the various interaction methods and design approaches within XR, and to understand how these technologies can address limitations found in traditional, physical trading environments. I am particularly interested in how XR can bridge the gap between digital and physical spaces to create more intuitive and effective user experiences.

My Learning and Execution Process

Our team spent the first day brainstorming ideas but initially struggled to reach a consensus due to the broad scope of the project topic. Eventually, one of my teammates, Luffy, proposed focusing on second-hand furniture trading, highlighting several issues he had observed in existing systems. Since we couldn't settle on a better idea at the time, we agreed to explore this direction further. We began by distributing a survey to gather user insights and received 17 responses. Interestingly, around 90% of respondents had experience buying or selling second-hand furniture in Hong Kong, confirming the relevance of our chosen theme. From their qualitative feedback, we identified five major challenges: size and fit issues, quality and condition, logistics and delivery, pricing concerns, and trust and communication. Our project aimed to address the first three challenges through XR technologies.

To better understand user's needs, we asked participants about their expectations. The top needs include the ability to visualize furniture in their own space and to preview 3D models using AR instead of static photos. To validate these findings, we visited a physical furniture store near Prince Edward Station. This visit confirmed several pain points, such as the time cost of traveling and the inability to preview how furniture would look in a home setting. We then moved into the ideation phase, using mind maps and user personas to shape our solution. We also analyzed existing platforms through storyboarding and conducted speed dating sessions with potential users to refine our concept.

Once we finalized our idea, we transitioned to prototyping. We built a virtual marketplace using Unity and published it on Spatial.io. To support sellers, we developed a mobile app that converts furniture photos into 3D models, which can then be uploaded to the virtual market. Buyers can download these models and use AR to visualize them in their own rooms, improving their understanding of the furniture's condition and fit. After completing our prototype, we conducted usability testing. The feedback was largely positive—users found the concept innovative and practical for solving the identified issues. However, some noted that the AR features could be improved in terms of accuracy, which we recognized as an area for future enhancement.

My Personal Contributions and Achievements

In this project, my primary responsibility was centered around the empathy phase, where I focused on understanding user needs and translating them into actionable insights for our solution. I designed and distributed a Google Form to collect responses from potential users, and then systematically organized and analyzed the feedback to identify key pain points and expectations. This process helped us define a user-centric direction for our platform.

To evaluate existing solutions and identify areas for improvement, I applied the GOMS (Goals, Operators, Methods, and Selection rules) model. This allowed us to compare the efficiency and usability of current platforms and extract both their strengths and limitations. These insights directly informed the design of our own solution, ensuring it addressed common user frustrations while incorporating effective features from existing systems.

Additionally, I contributed to the ideation phase by constructing a detailed mind map that helped visualize and organize potential features for our platform. This collaborative process enabled our team to explore a wide range of ideas and refine them into a cohesive and innovative solution. Through these contributions, I gained valuable experience in user research, interaction analysis, and early-stage design thinking.

My Personal Reflection

Through this project, I gained valuable insights into the potential of Extended Reality (XR) within the field of Human-Computer Interaction. I learned how XR technologies, such as AR and VR, can be applied to solve real-world problems that traditional physical solutions alone cannot address—particularly in areas like visualization, spatial understanding, and user engagement. This experience deepened my appreciation for the unique strengths of XR in enhancing user experiences and bridging the gap between digital and physical environments.

Additionally, I developed a better understanding of how to design XR solutions that are both user-friendly and effective. From conducting user research to prototyping and testing, I learned the importance of grounding design decisions in real user needs and feedback. This project not only strengthened my technical and analytical skills but also helped me grow as a computer engineer who is more empathetic, thoughtful, and solution oriented.
